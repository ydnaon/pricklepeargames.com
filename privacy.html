Privacy Policy — Doodle Defender

Prickle Pear Games · Effective July 4, 2026

The short version: Doodle Defender is an offline, single-player game. It collects no personal data — no accounts, no analytics, no ads, no tracking, and it does not even request internet access.

Data we collect

None. Doodle Defender does not collect, store, transmit, sell, or share any personal information. The app has no internet permission, so it is technically incapable of sending data off your device.

Data stored on your device

Your game progress — high scores, achievements, unlocked cosmetics, and settings — is saved only in the app's private storage on your own device. Deleting the app deletes this data.

The save-code feature

The optional Save Code tool in Settings copies your game progress to your device's clipboard as a text code, so you can back it up or move it to another device. This happens only when you tap the button, the code contains only game statistics (never personal information), and where you paste it is entirely up to you. The app never transmits it anywhere.

Android backup

Depending on your device settings, Android may include the game's save data in your personal Google account backup (Android Auto Backup). That backup belongs to you and is governed by Google's terms; we have no access to it.

App permissions

  • Vibrate — haptic feedback during play.
  • Wake lock — keeps the screen awake during a run.

The app requests no other permissions.

Children

The game collects no data from anyone, including children.

Changes to this policy

If the game ever changes in a way that affects privacy (for example, adding online features), this page will be updated before that version is released, with a new effective date.
